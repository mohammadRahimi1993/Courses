# 🧠 Basic-Exercise-Natural-Language-Processing (NLP-2022)
<p align = "justify"> Welcome to the Basic NLP Exercises repository. This collection was prepared in 2022 by Mohammad Rahimi, a PhD candidate at the University of Isfahan, Iran, and is designed to help learners become familiar with the fundamental concepts and practical exercises of Natural Language Processing (NLP). These exercises are highly recommended for students and beginners who want to start their journey in NLP and build a strong foundation in text processing and language technologies.To view the training course, please visit https://programmingschool.ir. </p>

## 📚 NLP Exercises

**Part 1: Word Frequency Analysis**  
In this tutorial, we extract a Wikipedia article and perform word frequency analysis on the Persian text to identify the most common terms.  
[Watch Part 1](https://www.aparat.com/v/o88m2g4)

**Part 2: Web Scraping Content**  
This video demonstrates how to programmatically extract and retrieve the full text content of a Persian Wikipedia article.  
[Watch Part 2](https://www.aparat.com/v/Kwv82)

**Part 3: Advanced Frequency Counting**  
We delve deeper into text processing by implementing advanced techniques to calculate and visualize word frequencies within a given corpus.  
[Watch Part 3](https://www.aparat.com/v/r00891m)

**Part 4: Pattern Extraction (Regex)**  
This tutorial focuses on text pattern matching, specifically demonstrating how to extract usernames from email addresses using Regular Expressions (Regex).  
[Watch Part 4](https://www.aparat.com/v/Am6ha)

**Part 5: Lemmatization with SpaCy**  
Learn how to process English text using the SpaCy library, focusing on lemmatization to reduce words to their base or root forms.  
[Watch Part 5](https://www.aparat.com/v/Qix7E)

**Part 6: Tokenization with Hazm**  
An introduction to the Hazm library for Persian NLP, specifically covering the tokenization process to segment sentences and words effectively.  
[Watch Part 6](https://www.aparat.com/v/DQ137)

**Part 7: Advanced Text Segmentation**  
Building upon the previous session, we continue exploring the Hazm library to perform sophisticated text segmentation and processing tasks.  
[Watch Part 7](https://www.aparat.com/v/n352q7h)
